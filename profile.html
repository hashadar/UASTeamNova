University Societies

I am actively involved in a number of societies at University. For second year, I decided to run for committee positions at three societies. Currently, I am the President of UCL Photo Society, Publicity Officer for UCL Stage Crew and part of the Events Team at UCL Engineering Society.

At UCL Photo Society, my primary goal is to continue the excellent and engaging activities in a safe manner. Due to the ongoing pandemic, in-person events must be kept to a minimum. Hence, a lot of events will need to take place online. My committee and I have continued the society activities with brand new event formats, competitions, workshops, socials and more.

I have taken part in a number of shows whilst at UCL Stage Crew; which can be seen below. Primarily, I work in production management and sound design. These technical skills are complimented by strong teamwork, time management and hard work. Technical Skills

As part of my degree, we are taught to use a variety of engineering software. I use CATIA V5 and Autodesk Fusion 360 for CAD. CATIA V5 is a very powerful piece of software, which is employed in a variety of industries. Autodesk Fusion 360 is also a very interesting piece of software, with functionality such as generative design and stress/strain modelling. I have used both pieces of software for coursework and personal projects alike, most recently designing a line launcher in CATIA V5 as part of the First Year Scenarios 1 project.

I also use MATLAB to create computer models with data. However, I have also been learning C++ as a hobby and building mini projects with the language. C++ is a powerful language which is also used in micro-controllers such as Arduinos, Drone Piloting computers and vehicle ECUs.

I use LaTeX to write my technical reports and notes. A powerful piece of typesetting software, LaTeX has proved to be a powerful tool in creating professional looking documents.
